
**Focused mode** - when you concentrate intently on something you're trying to learn to understand

**Diffuse mode** - a more relaxed thinking style related to a set of neural resting states

Focused mode is when you already have a familiar thought pattern. You're ​able to figure out the problem you're trying to solve, or the concept you're trying to understand that's ​related to something you're rather familiar with.

For concepts you haven't thought of before, to get to the new thought pattern, diffuse mode is needed. You can look ​at things broadly from a very different, big-picture perspective. You can make new neural connections traveling along new pathways. You can't focus in as tightly as you often ​need to, to finalize any kind of problem solving, or understand the finest aspects of a concept, but you can at least get to the initial place ​you need to be in to home in on a solution.

You're either in the focused mode or the diffuse mode of thinking. You can't be in both mode of thinking at the same time.

Analogies & metaphors provide powerful techniques for learning.

Learning something difficult takes time. The brain needs to alternate its ways of learning as it grapples with and assimilates the new material.

## What is Learning?

When you learn something new, many new synapses (connections) are formed on the dendrites of neurons.

There are a million billion synapses in the brain where memories are stored.
